Welcome to Civil Alliance for Climate Protection and Education!

At CACPE, we are dedicated to creating innovative and sustainable solutions that address Africa's Climate change challenges.

ABOUT

Climate change has become one of the major challenges of the 21st Century. The rate of unprecedented changes in our climate has adversely affected the Global community such that the theme has gained serious momentum of attention from intellectuals, decisions makers, environmental activists and donor agencies.
Civil Alliance for Climate Protection and Education (CACPE) is a Nigerian based Non-Profit dedicated to Climate action and Environmental Sustainability through targeted initiative in Clean energy, Climate-smart Agriculture, Advocacy, Land restoration, Waste management, Biodiversity conservation etc; we work with partners to push for impactful, scalable change. Since its inception, CACPE has reached over 9,300 individuals and planted over 7,000 trees, with 48 Staffs and 110 Volunteers across Nigeria. CACPE collaborates closely with Stakeholders like State and Federal Ministry of Environments, relevant CBOs and CSOs, including Youth and Marginalized groups, to build resilient communities capable of addressing environmental challenges. Our projects focus on Community-based solutions that are in line with global sustainability goals.

PROF. NKIRU THERESA MELUDU

CHAIRMAN BOARD OF TRUSTEES

Prof. Nkiru Theresa Meludu (STA, NF, OKP) is a Professor of Food and Nutrition Security/ Agricultural Extension at Nnamdi Azikiwe university, Awka, Anambra State, Nigeria. She earned her Ph.D in 1998 and became a Professor in the year 2010 from the University of Ibadan. She served as the Head of Department (2017-2021) and Dean, Faculty of Agriculture (2021-2023). Her research focuses on Organic Agriculture, Climate Change, Gender issues, Communication for Change, and Livestock Extension.
She has supervised 32 MSc and 8 Ph.D. students and published over 120 research articles, book chapters and books. She is the President of the Agricultural Extension Society of Nigeria and Home Economics Professional Association of Nigeria. With International Certification including courses from the Netherlands and Ghana, a Post doctoral fellowship in Japan, and attendance at over 10 international conferences, She is a member of National and International Associations and serves on several Boards of trustees.

PROF. RABIA SA'ID

BOARD MEMBER

Professor Rabia Sa’id is a distinguished Atmospheric Physicist with expertise in climate change dynamics, air pollution monitoring, air quality assessment, renewable energy, and atmospheric dynamics, including space weather. She is a faculty member in the Department of Physics and a Research Fellow at the Center for Renewable Energy and Sustainability Transitions (CREST) at Bayero University, Kano (BUK). She holds a PhD, MSc, and BSc in Physics from BUK, along with an MSc in Environment and Development from the University of Reading, UK. In 2020, Professor Sa’id became a Fellow at the James Martin Center for Nonproliferation Studies in Monterey, California, specializing in nuclear nonproliferation and security. She received a Civilian Research and Development Foundation (CRDF) grant in 2021 to promote peaceful nuclear applications and empower women in nuclear security. Currently, she is leading a STEM Outreach program (2024–2025), supported by the U.S. Mission in Nigeria’s Public Diplomacy Section.

PROJECTS

Projects Completed

Climate Change Awareness

Empowering communities and schools, we inspire the next generation of environmental advocates through dynamic climate clubs and impactful workshops. Our programs equip educators, leaders, and community members with essential environmental knowledge, fostering awareness and action. By building environmental literacy, we promote sustainable practices, drive community engagement, and encourage collective efforts to address climate challenges, paving the way for a greener, more resilient future.

Tree Planting & Refforestation

CACPE State Team teamed up with Ramat Polytechnic Student Union and Zulum Go Green organization to tackle climate change and promote environmental sustainability in Maiduguri, Borno State. We embarked on an ambitious tree planting and reforestation project, spreading awareness and inspiring action among our youth. Our impact? Over 500 trees planted, reducing carbon footprint and cleansing the air we breathe! We also empowered over 300 students and community members with knowledge on environmental conservation. Together, we're cultivating a brighter future for Nigeria - one tree, one community at a time.

Awareness on Clean Energy

Empowering Our Future Leaders with Clean Energy. At CACPE, we inspire a sustainable future for Nigeria's next generation. Our State Team partnered with Government Girls Secondary Schools Bauchi. We introduced clean energy initiatives and promoted environmental stewardship. Climate change is real; collective action is necessary. We trained 200+ students and teachers on clean energy benefits. Climate change awareness and sustainable practices were emphasized. This collaboration enhanced education through sustainability. Empowering Nigerian youth to become sustainable leaders.

Collaborative environmental Governance

Fostering Environmental Sustainability in Abia State. At CACPE, we promote collaborative governance for a greener Nigeria. Our State Team engaged with the Ministry of Environment, Abia State, to advance sustainable practices. We facilitated policy advocacy and stakeholder engagement, bringing together government, private sector, and civil society. Climate action requires collective effort. We emphasized environmental conservation, solid waste management, and erosion control. This partnership enhanced environmental governance and sustainability awareness. Empowering Abia State residents to drive positive change.

Youth Environmental Empowerment

Empowering Students, Restoring the Environment, CACPE partners with UNIZIK Demonstration Secondary School Awka to enhance environmental sustainability, mitigate climate change. We reclaim degraded land, promote eco-friendly practices and planted 200+ trees. We improved school landscape and biodiversity, enhanced environmental awareness and contributed to climate change mitigation, empowering Anambra's youth to drive positive change.

Climate Education Empowerment

Empowering Katsina Communities through Climate Education, CACPE conducted an environmental literacy workshop at Government College Katsina to enhance climate resilience through education and build capacity for sustainable practices. 200+ community members, 50+ educators, 20+ local leaders, and school staff participated. We discussed on climate change impacts and mitigation, sustainable agriculture, waste management & recycling and environmental advocacy. We enhanced environmental awareness among 300+ participants, community-led initiatives established and partnerships with local stakeholders strengthened.

Youth Climate Leadership

Supporting Abia's Youth for a Greener Future. At CACPE, we're cultivating environmental stewards among Nigeria's next generation. Our Abia State Team established climate clubs at Santa Cruz Secondary School, inspiring students to become eco-leaders. We educated and empowered the youth on climate action to build capacity for sustainable practices and environmental advocacy. We trained 200+ students on climate change mitigation, established school and community climate clubs.

Strategic Climate Partnership with American Space

Scaling Climate Action through Strategic Partnerships. At CACPE, we're amplifying climate impact through collaborative efforts. Our Bauchi State Team forged a strategic partnership with American Space, convening key stakeholders to drive climate action. The partnership was aimed to enhance collective impact on climate mitigation and adaptation to foster synergy among stakeholders for local, national, regional, and global climate action. We engaged 50+ stakeholders in environment (ShE) sector, we facilitated workshops on climate best practices and principles, established a climate action network for knowledge sharing and developed joint initiatives for sustainable development resulted to strengthened partnerships with ShE stakeholders, enhanced capacity for climate action among stakeholders and amplified climate advocacy and awareness in Bauchi State.

Celebrating Climate Collaboration

At CACPE we honour exceptional Partnership, as part of our mission, we value collaborative efforts driving climate action. Our Katsina State Team presented certificates of appreciation to ACReSAL staff, celebrating a fruitful partnership. We recognized ACReSAL's dedication to climate action, acknowledged joint initiatives' success and strengthened ties with key stakeholders. Throughout the partnership, we've successfully enhanced climate resilience in Katsina State, improved capacity building for sustainable land management and strengthened partnerships for environmental conservation.
